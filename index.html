Sahara

In the heart of North Africa, where the sun reigns supreme and the land stretches endlessly, lies the Sahara Desert, a vast expanse of sand dunes, rocky plateaus, and ancient oases. Spanning over 9 million square kilometers.

Western Desert

The Western Desert, also known as the Libyan Desert, is a vast region located in North Africa, encompassing parts of Egypt and Libya. It is one of the most arid and desolate landscapes on Earth, characterized by vast expanses of sand dunes, rocky plateaus, and barren plains.

Al Bagawat

The Al-Bagawat Necropolis is one of the oldest and best-preserved Christian cemeteries in the world, dating back to the 4th century AD. It is a testament to the early Christian presence in Egypt and the resilience of the Christian community in the face of persecution.

Pyramids Of Giza

Located on the Giza Plateau, just outside Cairo, Egypt, the Pyramids of Giza are comprised of three main pyramids: the Great Pyramid of Khufu (also known as Cheops), the Pyramid of Khafre (Chephren), and the Pyramid of Menkaure (Mycerinus).

Kalahari Desert

The Kalahari Desert is a vast semi-arid sandy savannah that covers much of Botswana, as well as parts of Namibia and South Africa. It extends over 900,000 square kilometers (350,000 square miles) and is surrounded by other African ecosystems, including the Okavango Delta and the Namib Desert.
